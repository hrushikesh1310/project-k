Types of AI by Capability

Capability = how broadly the system can solve tasks

This lens answers: “How many different kinds of problems can it handle?” Most AI today is strong at one narrow job.

Narrow AI (ANI)

Good at a specific task (or a small set of tasks). It can look “smart” but it’s not generally intelligent.

Exists today Most products
Examples: spam filters, translation, face recognition, route planning, chess engines, music recommendations.

General AI (AGI)

Human-level ability across many tasks: learning new skills, reasoning, and adapting like a person.

Not achieved Research / debate
Reality check: We do not currently have a confirmed, widely agreed AGI.

Superintelligence (ASI)

Smarter than the best humans in nearly everything (science, creativity, social skills, strategy).

Hypothetical Sci‑fi + philosophy
Why discuss it? It helps people think about safety, control, and long-term impacts.

Quick comparison

What narrow AI can do

  • Beat you at a game
  • Detect patterns in photos
  • Summarize text
  • Predict what you might like next

What it usually can’t do

  • Understand goals like a human
  • Handle any task you give it
  • Know when it’s uncertain
  • Be “wise” or “ethical” by default
Pro tip: When someone says “this product uses AI”, ask: Is it narrow or general? and what task exactly?
