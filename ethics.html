Use & Ethics

Because “smart” tools still need smart users

AI can help a lot—while also creating risks. This page is a simple guide to using AI responsibly.

1) Bias

AI learns patterns from data. If the data contains unfair patterns, the AI can repeat them.

Example: a hiring tool trained mostly on one group might unfairly score others lower.

2) Privacy

If you share private info with an AI tool, you might be giving it to a company, a model, or a dataset.

Rule of thumb: Don’t paste passwords, private addresses, medical info, or secrets.

3) Hallucinations (confident mistakes)

Some AI systems can create answers that look correct but aren’t. This is common in text generation.

How to handle: ask for sources, cross-check, and verify important facts.

4) Deepfakes & misinformation

AI can generate realistic images, audio, and videos. That can be fun—but also dangerous.

Quick check: If it triggers a strong emotion, pause and verify before sharing.

How to be a smart AI user (checklist)

Do

  • Use AI as a helper, not the final judge.
  • Double-check important answers (school, health, money, law).
  • Ask: “What could be wrong with this?”
  • Give feedback when tools allow it.

Don’t

  • Assume it’s always correct.
  • Share sensitive info.
  • Use it to cheat in a way that hurts your learning.
  • Trust deepfakes without verification.
Big idea: The most powerful safety feature is still… you.
