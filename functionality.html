Types of AI by Functionality

Functionality = what “mind-like” abilities it has

This lens answers: “How does it behave internally?” Does it only react? Does it use memory? Can it model other minds?

The spectrum

Reactive Machines

They respond to the current input only. No long-term memory of the past.
Example vibe: a chess program that evaluates the board right now.

Limited Memory

Uses past information (a short history) to make better decisions. Many modern systems fit here.
Example vibe: a driving system that tracks nearby cars over time.

Theory of Mind (research)

The idea: the AI understands that other beings have thoughts, feelings, and intentions (like humans do). This is still research.

Self-aware (hypothetical)

A system that has a sense of “self” and internal experience. There’s no confirmed self-aware AI today.

Reality check: When people say “AI is conscious”, they usually mean it talks well—not that it’s truly self-aware.

Where do chatbots fit?

Many chatbots look like they “understand” you, but a lot of that is powerful pattern recognition on language.

Good question to ask: Does it track context reliably? Does it know when it’s unsure? Can it check sources?

Why this classification matters

It helps us avoid confusion:

  • “Reactive” systems can be fast and reliable but not flexible.
  • “Limited memory” systems can adapt but may have new failure modes.
  • “Theory of mind / self-aware” claims should require strong evidence.
