Glossary

Short definitions for quick understanding

If a term feels fuzzy, check here. Keep it simple, then go deeper when you’re ready.

AI (Artificial Intelligence)

Systems that perform tasks we consider “smart” (recognize, predict, decide).

Model

A pattern-finder that turns inputs (data) into outputs (predictions/actions).

Dataset

A collection of examples used to train or evaluate a model.

Training

Adjusting a model so it performs better—usually by learning from many examples.

Inference

When a trained model is used to make a prediction on new input.

Bias

Unfair patterns in data or decisions that can lead to unequal outcomes.

Hallucination

When an AI generates confident output that is incorrect or unsupported.

Prompt

The text/instructions you give an AI model to guide its output.

LLM (Large Language Model)

A model trained on lots of text to predict and generate language.

Overfitting

When a model memorizes training examples and performs worse on new data.
